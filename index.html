Shakira's Artistry

Shakira Isabel Mebarak Ripoll was born the 2nd of February in 1977.
Born in Barranquilla Colombia, she has achieved stardom in the Spanish and English demographic.

At the beginning of the young singers career which began at the age of 13, her first two studio albums did not perform well. Shakira has released a total of 11 studio albums along with a few live album recordings. Her first English album Laundry Service did very well in the United States debuting at number 3 on the Billboard 200. She received help from Gloria Estefan who helped her learn English and worked hard for this album. Her smash hit single "Waka Waka (This Time for Africa)" sold more than a million copies in the United States. Her sixth studio album Fijación Oral, Vol. 1 became the best-selling Latin pop album and second best selling Latin album in general of the 200's in the U.S.

Achievements

Shakira is a 3x Grammy Award winner along with being a 12x Latin Grammy Award winner. She has sold more than 75 million records across the globe. The only artist from South America to reach number 1 on the Billboard Hot 100 chart. In 2010, Shakira's music was the most downloaded than any other Latino artist in the past five years according to Nokia. Along with that, in 2011 she was granted and honored at the Latin Grammys with Latin Recording Academy Person of the Year. She currently has a star on the Hollywood Walk of Fame!

The list of awards she earned is magnificant and unreal. In 2012, she was acknowledged by the Chevalier De L'ordre des Arts et des Lettres. In the same year Forbes had ranked Shakira amongst the Top 100 World's Most Powerful Women. In 2014, Shakira became the first musical act to perform at the FIFA World Cup three times. A fun fact is that there was a new species of a wasp discovered and was named after her (Aleiodes shaakirae).

"In 2018, Spotify included Shakira in the list of the top 10 most streamed female artists of the decade on the platform, making her the highest streamed Latin artist. In 2020, Shakira became the first female artist to have 4 songs from different decades to have over 100 million streams on Spotify, also marking her as the only artist with Spanish songs, the only Latin artist, and third overall after Michael Jackson and Eminem to achieve this milestone."

Legacy and Impact

It is no doubt that Shakira is a huge icon/figure in the Latin music. New York Times has even gone far enough to call her the "Titan of Latin Pop." In 2010, Google came out saying that Shakira was the most searched female entertainer of that year. A handful of artists have named Shakira an influence such as Taylor Swift, Rihanna, Katy Perry, Beyoncé, etc. In a 2017 article by Fortune, she was named one of the "World's Greatest Leaders".

According to Britannica Academic, in 2003 Shakira became a goodwill ambassador for UNICEF. By being an ambassador, she was able to bring into a better light the struggles that children in poorer countries are facing. Along with that she had created an organization called the Pies Descalzos Foundation (named after her third studio album) which centered on violence against children in Colombia. The foundation focused on five schools across Colombia which helped aid and feed 4,000 kids. The World Literacy Foundation made an announcement that Shakira will be awarded the 2020 Global Literacy Award for "her significant contribution to the improvement of literacy for disadvantaged children around the world."

Shakira had an article written from The Guardian about how her impact on Colombia's education and bringing social awareness. She was fortunate enough to sit down and communicate these issues with Barack Obama. She has used her platform to not only be a pop music icon, but to also spread her political messages. Her influence clearly is not limited to a bubble, she is an inspiration and ilustrious.
